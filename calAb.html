Derivitives

The point of derivitives (at least for me) is to find the slope of an equation.

While you could use the slope equation for simple lines, how are you suppose to get the slope of an equation like: y = cos(x)/sin(x)?

Well obviously you use calculus or else I'd cut this part out.

As for other functions that have multiple parts that have different equations, then you have to break it down and derive all of them.

But I'm getting lazy so I'll just explain everything here now.

LET'S GET STARTED SHALL WE?

Imagine a really amazing function, y = x. The slope is one right? Well yes, because the slope is constant.

Now imaging another really iconic function, y = x2. This slope is constantly either increasing or decreasing, until the point of inflection.

Don't know what a point of inflection is? Don't worry, that's for later.

As I previously stated, Derivitives are all about getting the slope of a specific part of any funciton and there are some basic rules to learn.

The Power Rule

It honestly is way lamer than it sounds, but you basically look at a function like y = x4. And do this to it.

dy/dx = 4x3

Do you see what I did? Hopefully you do because typing without a wrist rest sucks so I'm going on.


Now look at a function like y = x1/2. This obviously is a square root function, and you apply the POWER RULE.

Just watch and learn about how stupid this thing is.

dy/dx = -1/2x1/2

Do you have no idea what happened? Good, because that's what's suppose to happen.

Let's break this down shall we?

First, get a piece of notebook paper out because this stuff is intense.

Okay, so first you can multiply the function y by the power, which is 1/2 in this case.

Second, you subtract one from the exponent, which makes -1/2.

Since a negative exponent does this: 1/x, you can now hopefully figure out how I got that answer

If you didn't then just ask somebody else because I don't have a degree in education...


Addition, Multiplying, & Dividing

Now for other more complicated equations like y = x25x

I'm just going to show the answer right below so you can try to guess what happened

dy/dx = 5x2 + 10x

Before you people say anything, just know that this is an example and if you noticed that the equation can be rewritten to y = 5x3, then good for you.

The point of this is to show you how to derive multiplying equations, if you actually want to know why, then you should watch 3blue1brown becuse he does it better.

But you're here anyways, so I'll just give you a pneumonic so you can remember how to derive it.

left d-right, right d-left

You just get the left side of the equation, and multiply it by the derivitive of the right side, and the same concept applies to the other side.

Looking at the equation again, you should know be able to see how I got the answer, and using the power rule and some re-writting can show that this is the right answer.
